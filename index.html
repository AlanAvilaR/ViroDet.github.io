Introduction

The world is facing one of the most critical challenges from all the history, and everyone needs to take part in taking action for solving all the problems derived from SARS-CoV-2. Without a vaccine or appropriate medical treatment in sight, countries in regions such as Latin America are positioning themselves to be the worst hit during the ongoing COVID-19 Pandemic.



  • Regional Health System: Increase the diagnostic capacity of clinical units for this pandemic and those to come.
  • Citizen Response: Unreliable case numbers and even suppression of data have caught the attention of the community.
  • Vulnerable Communities: Prioritize roadmaps that include communities that do not have laboratories equipped to diagnose.

ViroDet seeks to develop, validate and implement:

  1. A low-cost and accessible diagnostic module that integrates a real-time PCR thermal cycler.
  2. Two diagnostic test kits for the detection of SARS-CoV-2 with molecular techniques of RT-qPCR and RT-LAMP based on open-source technologies and endemic materials from Mexico.

PCR & Results

Step 01

Nasopharyngeal cotton swab swab is inserted into nostril to absorb secretions. Specimen is stored at 2-8°C for up to 72 hours. RNA human samples are obtained from certified authorities.

Step 02

Samples are added to our reaction kits which contain all the necessary enzymes and probes for detecting SARS-CoV-2. Reaction mixes are added to the device. Our device is capable of running 16 samples in parallel.

Step 03

Depending on the desired test, users can obtain a result between 1-2 hours. Results can be seen on the screen of the device and can be stored for later analysis.

Expected Results

  1. ViroDet leads this effort with a focus on low-middle income countries.
  2. Monitoring to better develop and implement appropriate strategies.
  3. Allows us to take preventive measures that reduce the virus spreading.
  4. Consequently, this will avoid saturation of public and private hospitals.

A community effort on COVID-19 diagnostics for Mexico and Latin America

ViroDet's goals are to develop open-source diagnostic tools approved by health regulatory institutions so that it can be adopted and used in clinics in vulnerable communities. Likewise, we seek that our technology can be easily replicated in other Latin American countries.
